\name{Weiping Yan\thanks{Code, proofs, per-clip score arrays, and locked environments:
  \texttt{github.com/\allowbreak appleweiping/\allowbreak resynthesis-watermark-limits}.}}
\address{University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, Minneapolis, MN, USA}

\begin{abstract}
Speech watermarks are widely reported to fail when audio is regenerated by a vocoder or
neural codec. We give this phenomenon a restricted but precise model: a blind launderer is
an \emph{analysis--resynthesis channel} $\W=\mathcal S\circ\A$. At full generality we prove
only what is true: a data-processing inequality (laundering cannot increase the Chernoff
detection exponent), and an \emph{exact-erasure condition} --- if embedding leaves the
analysis output unchanged a.s., the laundered laws coincide and no detector beats chance.
In a linear-Gaussian surrogate we obtain closed forms: the channel transmits exactly the
equivalence class of the perturbation modulo $\ker\A$, with surviving exponent
$\tfrac18\norm{P\delta}^2$; a Gaussian \emph{achievable lower bound} $\Rlb$ (not a
capacity) covers blind embedders. The model's empirical content is a measurable
\emph{survival predictor}: geometric quantities --- each attacker's own analysis
sensitivity, or a static magnitude-change fraction. On LibriSpeech ($\nTest$
speaker-disjoint test clips, thresholds from $\nCalib$ independent calibration clips),
across five resynthesis families (spectral inversion, a neural vocoder, and three neural
codecs), these predict held-out post-attack separability \emph{for a fixed mel-reading
detector} within every attacker (pooled Spearman $\spearmanSens$, perm.\ $p\permP$),
while SNR and spectral-centroid controls fail --- survival is a property of the (channel,
detector-domain) pair: for a waveform-correlation detector two of the three codecs
(EnCodec, SNAC) reverse the sign. Deployed watermarks (AudioSeal, WavMark, SilentCipher),
each at its native transparent strength, lose their calibrated operating point under
nearly every lossy channel, in modes we keep separate: \emph{erasure} (separability itself
collapses), \emph{calibration failure} (separability remains but the fixed threshold's
false-alarm rate explodes on attacked clean audio), and \emph{graceful degradation}
(recall falls but detection still works).
\end{abstract}

audio watermarking, generative AI, provenance, data-processing inequality, hypothesis testing

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
Provenance watermarking embeds a key-dependent signal in speech so a detector can later
flag synthetic or traced audio~\cite{sanroman2024audioseal,chen2023wavmark,
singh2024silentcipher}. A consistent empirical finding is fragility to
\emph{regeneration}: passing watermarked audio through a vocoder, neural codec, or voice
conversion drives deployed detectors toward failure~\cite{oreilly2025shallow,wen2025sok}.
The community's response is to embed in representations that resynthesis preserves ---
codec latents (Latent-Mark~\cite{chen2026latentmark}), speech-feature-aligned
marks~\cite{li2026featurealigned}, speaker-latents robust to zero-shot
VC~\cite{li2025voicemark}, and semantic image watermarks before
them~\cite{zhao2024invisible,wen2023treering,yang2024gaussianshading}. The
\emph{invariant-alignment idea is thus established practice, not our contribution.}

What the literature lacks is a stated model in which the collapse and its exceptions can
be derived. Empirical papers report which schemes die under which attacks; the pattern
--- AudioSeal survives EnCodec yet fails under mel-inversion; marks with more energy in
preserved components survive longer --- is left as observation. This paper supplies the
missing object: a channel model with exactly-scoped guarantees that \emph{organizes} the
deployed-watermark phenomenology (E1), and a \emph{measurable} geometric quantity that,
in a controlled setting (constructed marks, a fixed detector), \emph{predicts}
post-attack separability out-of-sample (E2). We do not claim the predictor numerically
forecasts a given deployed scheme's survival; the two experiments are complementary.

\noindent\textbf{Contributions.}
(i)~A general proposition for blind analysis--resynthesis channels: a Chernoff
data-processing inequality, and a sufficient condition for \emph{exact} erasure
(\S\ref{sec:model}). Nothing stronger is claimed at this generality.
(ii)~A linear-Gaussian surrogate where everything is closed-form: the channel transmits
the perturbation's class in $\R^n/\ker\A$; mixed perturbations survive \emph{partially}
with exponent $\tfrac18\norm{P\delta}^2$ (\S\ref{sec:lg}).
(iii)~A Gaussian achievable lower bound $\Rlb$ for blind embedders --- explicitly
\emph{not} a watermarking capacity: informed embedding~\cite{costa1983writing,
moulin2003information} can exceed it and no rate converse is claimed (\S\ref{sec:lg}).
(iv)~The finding that \emph{geometric} quantities --- each attacker's own analysis
sensitivity $s_\W$, or a static magnitude-change fraction --- predict \emph{mel-domain}
post-attack detectability on \emph{held-out, speaker-disjoint} data across five
resynthesis families (while budget/centroid controls do not), with the sign reversing for
EnCodec/SNAC in the waveform-detector domain --- prediction is detector-domain-specific
(\S\ref{sec:exp}).
(v)~A calibrated operational evaluation of deployed watermarks, separating loss of
\emph{separability} (oriented AUC) from loss of the \emph{calibrated operating point}
(TPR and \emph{achieved} FPR at a threshold fixed on an independent calibration set) ---
distinctions routinely conflated (\S\ref{sec:exp}).

\section{The analysis--resynthesis channel}
\label{sec:model}
A watermark embedder $E_\kappa$ maps a host $x\sim P_0$ to $E_\kappa(x)$ (additively,
$E_\kappa(x)=x+\delta_\kappa$), giving the watermarked law $P_1$. A detector knowing
$\kappa$ tests $H_0\!:P_0$ vs $H_1\!:P_1$; with $N$ i.i.d.\ looks the optimal Bayes error
decays as $e^{-N\,C(P_0,P_1)}$, $C$ the Chernoff
information~\cite{chernoff1952measure,cover2006elements}. A \emph{blind launderer} is
\begin{equation}
\W \;=\; \mathcal S\circ \A,
\end{equation}
where $\A$ maps a waveform to a representation $z$ (a mel envelope, a codec latent) and
$\mathcal S$ regenerates a waveform from $z$ with randomness independent of the input.
The attacker knows neither $\kappa$ nor the scheme (adaptive, key-aware removal
\cite{ding2026evade} is out of scope). Imperceptibility is a budget $\rho(\delta;x)\le D$.

\begin{proposition}[general channels]\label{prop:general}
For any channel $\W$,
\begin{equation*}
C(\W_\#P_0,\W_\#P_1)\;\le\;C(P_0,P_1).
\end{equation*}
If moreover $\A(E_\kappa(x))=\A(x)$ holds $P_0$-a.s.\ and $\mathcal S$ depends on its
input only through $z$, then $\W_\#P_1=\W_\#P_0$; hence
$C(\W_\#P_0,\W_\#P_1)=0$ and no detector beats chance at any sample size.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The first part is the DPI for the Chernoff family: each
$-\log\int p_0^{1-s}p_1^s$ is monotone under Markov kernels and the max over $s$
preserves monotonicity~\cite{cover2006elements}. For the second,
$\W(E_\kappa(x))=\mathcal S(\A(E_\kappa(x)),U)=\mathcal S(\A(x),U)=\W(x)$ a.s.\ with
$U\perp x$, so the output laws coincide.
\end{proof}

Proposition~\ref{prop:general} is all we assert for real nonlinear vocoders and codecs:
laundering cannot help the detector, and \emph{exactly} analysis-invariant embeddings are
\emph{exactly} erased. It supplies no constants and no prediction of partial survival.
Quantitative structure requires a model.

\section{Linear-Gaussian surrogate}
\label{sec:lg}
Work in whitened coordinates, $P_0=\N(0,I_n)$, perceptual weighting carried by a masking
metric $M\succ0$ ($\rho(\delta)=\delta^\top M\delta$). Let $\A\in\R^{k\times n}$ have full
row rank, $P=\A^{+}\A$ the orthogonal projector onto $\mathrm{row}(\A)$, and let synthesis
resample the complement from the clean prior:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lgchannel}
\W(x)=Px+(I-P)w,\qquad w\sim\N(0,I_n)\ \text{fresh}.
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[exact surviving shift and exponent]\label{thm:lg}
Under \eqref{eq:lgchannel}, for a fixed additive shift $\delta$:
$\W_\#\N(\delta,I)=\N(P\delta,I)$ and
\begin{equation}
C(\W_\#P_0,\W_\#P_1)\;=\;\tfrac18\norm{P\delta}^2 .
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

The channel transmits exactly the equivalence class $[\delta]\in\R^n/\ker\A$: the
effective perturbation is $P\delta$. Three consequences, stated carefully:
$\delta\in\ker\A$ is erased exactly (recovering Prop.~\ref{prop:general});
\emph{mixed} perturbations survive \emph{partially} (exponent
$\tfrac18\norm{P\delta}^2>0$ whenever $P\delta\neq0$); and row-space-only perturbations
waste no budget on erased components but are \emph{not} the unique surviving set. The
budget-optimal exponent is $\max_{\delta^\top M\delta\le D}\tfrac18\norm{P\delta}^2 =
\tfrac D8\,\lambda_{\max}(P,M)$, at the top generalized eigenvector of $(P,M)$.

\noindent\textbf{A rate lower bound (not capacity).} Writing surviving shifts in an
orthonormal basis of $\mathrm{row}(\A)$, a \emph{blind} (host-uninformed, Gaussian
codebook) embedder facing a $\kappa$-aware but host-blind decoder achieves any rate below
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rlb}
\Rlb=\max_{Q\succeq0,\ \tr(M_{\mathrm{row}}Q)\le D}\tfrac12\log\det(I+Q)
\end{equation}
nats per invariant coordinate (host as unit-variance interference; water-filling over
$M_{\mathrm{row}}$'s eigenmodes). $\Rlb$ is an \emph{achievable lower bound for that
restricted class}: informed (host-aware) embedding in the Costa/Moulin--O'Sullivan
sense~\cite{costa1983writing,moulin2003information} can exceed it, and we prove no rate
converse. A nullspace embedding has $\Rlb=0$ after $\W$.

\noindent\textbf{From surrogate to real channels.} For a real attacker $\W$ the model
suggests a \emph{measurable} channel-relative predictor: the attacker's own analysis
sensitivity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sens}
s_{\W}(\delta;x)=\norm{\A_{\W}(x+\delta)-\A_{\W}(x)}/\norm{\delta},
\end{equation}
computed with \emph{that} attacker's $\A_\W$ (a mel front-end, a codec encoder). Whether
$s_\W$ predicts post-attack separability is an \emph{empirical hypothesis} --- the
surrogate motivates it but does not prove it. Testing it out-of-sample is E2's job; a
single mel fraction is never reused across attackers with different $\A_\W$.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:exp}
\IfFileExists{tab_e1.tex}{\input{tab_e1}}{}
\noindent\textbf{Setup.} LibriSpeech 16\,kHz: test $=$ \nTest{} speaker-stratified
4\,s voice-active clips from test-clean (\nSpeakersTest{} speakers); detector thresholds
come \emph{only} from \nCalib{} independent calibration clips (dev-clean, dev-other, and
test-other, all speaker-disjoint from the test-clean evaluation set); the predictor's
mapping is fit on a dev-clean fit split and evaluated on test-clean only. Ten attackers:
STFT-GL (near-lossless control), mel-GL (spectral inversion of THE unified 80-mel
analysis), Vocos (neural vocoder), Vocos-EnCodec (learned reconstruction from codec
tokens), EnCodec at $6/3$\,kbps, DAC, SNAC, and kNN-VC self voice-conversion
(top-$k\in\{4,8\}$) --- content-, speaker- and prosody-preserving re-synthesis. The
\emph{predictor} study (E2) uses the five with a common analysis representation (mel-GL,
Vocos, EnCodec, DAC, SNAC). All formal runs fail loudly if any attacker or baseline is
missing. Scores, seeds, keys, manifests, and locked environments ship with the repository.

\noindent\textbf{Constructed marks (geometry probes).} W.r.t.\ the unified mel analysis
$\A$ we construct, per utterance and key: a \emph{kernel mark} (alternating projections
onto quadrature $\cap$ consistent spectrograms --- the first-order kernel of magnitude
analysis) and a \emph{row mark} (in-phase mel-pattern shifts). Construction quality is
\emph{measured}, not assumed: at the operating amplitude the kernel mark's analysis
change is only $\nullLeakOp\times$ that of a random direction of equal norm (residual
second-order leakage), whereas the row mark's is $\rowLeakOp\times$. Their detection uses a genie-aided matched-filter \emph{probe}
(the known per-utterance perturbation) --- a diagnostic of channel geometry, explicitly
not a deployable detector; deployed-detector conclusions come only from the baselines.

\noindent\textbf{E2 --- do geometric predictors predict survival?}
For 108 random directions (kernel/row mixtures, random in-phase) $\times$ randomized
budgets, we measure $s_\W$ \eqref{eq:sens} per attacker and post-attack detectability
(oriented probe AUC against host variability) in \emph{two detector domains}: waveform
correlation and a fixed mel-reading matched filter. Mappings are fit on dev-clean and
evaluated on test-clean only; all statistics are within-attacker (rank-pooled), so
between-attacker level differences can neither create nor mask an association.
Results (Fig.~\ref{fig:e2}): $s_\W$ predicts mel-domain detectability \emph{within
every attacker} (per-attacker Spearman $\rhoMelmelgl/\rhoMelvocos/\rhoMelencodec/
\rhoMeldac/\rhoMelsnac$; pooled $\spearmanSens$, permutation $p\permP$), as does the
static magnitude-change fraction ($\spearmanMagFrac$) --- both \emph{geometric}
quantities --- while budget (SNR, $\spearmanSNR$) and spectral centroid
($\spearmanCentroid$) do not. Empirically, the near-null regime behaves as the
exact-erasure condition (Prop.~\ref{prop:general}) would suggest: the $\necNLow$
directions with $s_\W\!\le\!0.25\times$ median (they exist only for the mel-analysis
channels --- the codecs' kernels are too small to reach) deviate from chance by median
$\necLowMel$, \emph{maximum} $\necMaxLowMel$, versus $\necRestMel$ for the rest --- an
observed monotonicity of the predictor, not a proof that Prop.~\ref{prop:general} holds
for these nonlinear channels. In the \emph{waveform} domain, two of the three codecs
show the \emph{opposite} dependence ($\rho_s=\rhoWaveEncodec$ EnCodec, $\rhoWaveSnac$
SNAC): they transmit precisely the sub-mel detail that mel analysis discards. (DAC keeps
the mel-domain sign, $\rho_s=\rhoWaveDac$, and mel-GL/Vocos go to chance.) Survival is
thus a property of the (channel, detector-domain) pair, not of the watermark alone.

\noindent\textbf{E1 --- deployed watermarks.}
AudioSeal~\cite{sanroman2024audioseal}, WavMark~\cite{chen2023wavmark}, and
SilentCipher~\cite{singh2024silentcipher}, each at its native full strength --- all in
the transparent regime but not equalized (clean-embed PESQ $\pesqAS/\pesqWM/\pesqSC$;
we do not force a common PESQ, which would push each scheme off its designed operating
point, and we note below that AudioSeal carries the most energy). Per (baseline,
attacker, key)
we report oriented AUC (utterance-clustered bootstrap CIs) and the calibrated operating
point --- TPR \emph{and achieved FPR} at the 1\%-FPR threshold fixed on \nCalib{}
independent clean negatives (Clopper--Pearson intervals) --- plus an attack-aware
recalibration diagnostic (Table~\ref{tab:e1}). We keep two questions separate: does
\emph{separability} survive (oriented AUC, CI vs.\ $0.5$) and does the \emph{calibrated
operating point} survive (TPR at the fixed-threshold's achieved FPR)? All three baselines
are perfect on the near-lossless STFT control. SilentCipher and WavMark lose \emph{both}
under all nine lossy channels (\emph{erasure}: AUC $0.99\!\to\!0.50$, TPR $\to\!0.00$,
unrecoverable). AudioSeal is more varied: separability is \emph{erased} only by SNAC and
self-VC (AUC $\to\!0.50$--$0.51$, CI covers $0.5$); under mel-inversion, Vocos and
Vocos-EnCodec it retains \emph{weak} separability (AUC $0.54$--$0.63$, CIs above $0.5$)
but the operating point is dead (TPR $\le0.07$); under EnCodec it keeps \emph{strong}
separability (AUC $0.93/0.81$) yet the fixed threshold's FPR explodes
($0.01\!\to\!0.73/0.80$) as the codec pushes \emph{clean} audio across the boundary (a
\emph{calibration failure}); and under DAC it merely \emph{degrades} (AUC $0.93$, FPR
$0.01$, recall $0.64$). Survival is channel-specific in the way the subspace picture
suggests --- what a scheme's detector reads, relative to what each channel preserves and
how it moves clean signals --- and no sign/order inversion occurred in the final protocol.

\noindent\textbf{E3 --- multi-bit proof of concept (no rate claim).}
An $8$-bit payload in the unified mel domain (frame-differential BPSK, fully
\emph{blind} decoder, PESQ $4.2$) is carried at BER $0.10$ under mel-GL and Vocos and
$0.17$ under EnCodec and DAC (clean $0.05$), but fails under SNAC (BER $0.47$); all with
binomial CIs. This demonstrates the invariant sub-channel carries bits through most
resynthesizers; it is \emph{not} an achievable-rate measurement and we claim no bits/s
at target reliability.

\section{Relation to prior work}
Attack studies document the collapse~\cite{oreilly2025shallow,wen2025sok}; invariant/
latent-aligned embedding is established practice: Latent-Mark optimizes waveforms to
shift codec latents with cross-codec transfer~\cite{chen2026latentmark}, Feature-Aligned
marks match speech-feature distributions~\cite{li2026featurealigned}, VoiceMark targets
VC-invariant speaker latents~\cite{li2025voicemark}; in images, semantic watermarks
realize the same prescription~\cite{zhao2024invisible,wen2023treering,
yang2024gaussianshading}. Relative to all of these we add what a method paper does not:
a stated channel model with exactly-scoped guarantees (Prop.~\ref{prop:general},
Thm.~\ref{thm:lg}), an explicit account of what is \emph{not} proven ($\Rlb$ vs
capacity~\cite{moulin2003information,costa1983writing,chen2001quantization}), and a
falsifiable, channel-relative predictor validated out-of-sample with permutation
controls. Our evaluation protocol (independent calibration, oriented-vs-raw separation)
addresses measurement pathologies that affect published robustness numbers.

\section{Scope and limitations}
\label{sec:disc}
The theory covers blind, non-adaptive laundering; key-aware
removal~\cite{ding2026evade} is out of scope. Exact erasure requires exact analysis
invariance --- our constructed kernel marks realize it only to first order, with
measured second-order leakage at operating amplitude. $\Rlb$ concerns a restricted
embedder class; real capacities may be higher. The surrogate's closed forms are not
claimed for nonlinear generators --- for those we claim only Prop.~\ref{prop:general}
and the \emph{measured} predictive power of $s_\W$. The three deployed baselines run at
native strength and are not PESQ-equalized (SI-SDR $\sisdrAS/\sisdrWM/\sisdrSC$\,dB);
AudioSeal carries the most mark energy, so we claim no cross-scheme robustness ranking;
the survival heterogeneity is \emph{within} AudioSeal (WavMark and SilentCipher run at
maximum strength yet are still fully erased, so energy cannot explain their collapse).
Survival statements are about oriented separability or calibrated operating points, never
raw sub-$0.5$ AUC read as erasure.

\section{Conclusion}
A restricted but precise subspace model of analysis--resynthesis explains, with
correctly-scoped guarantees, when speech watermarks survive laundering, and yields a
measurable geometric predictor that --- for a fixed mel-reading detector --- generalizes
across attackers and held-out speakers, while reversing in the waveform-detector domain:
survival is a property of the (channel, detector) pair. Honest accounting --- of what is
proved, what is measured, and what deployed detectors actually do under attack --- is the
paper's method as much as its message.
