\title{The Resynthesis Channel: Fundamental Limits of Speech\\
       Watermarking Against Generative Laundering}
\name{Weiping Yan\thanks{Code, proofs, and data:

\address{University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, Minneapolis, MN, USA}

\begin{abstract}
Audio watermarks are increasingly defeated when an attacker regenerates the signal through a
vocoder or codec; this ``regeneration'' collapse is widely documented, yet for speech there is
no theory of \emph{which} watermarks die, under \emph{which} attacker, or \emph{what can
survive}. We supply one. We model any blind analysis--resynthesis launderer
as a \emph{resynthesis channel} $\W=\mathcal{S}\circ\A$: an analysis map $\A$ that keeps
only the perceptual invariants of speech (content, speaker, prosody) and a synthesis map
$\mathcal{S}$ that regenerates a waveform, resampling everything $\A$ discarded. Two matched
results follow. A \emph{converse} (data-processing inequality): any watermark supported in
$\ker\A$---phase and psychoacoustically masked components, where imperceptibility drives
post-hoc schemes---has post-laundering detection error-exponent exactly zero. An
\emph{achievability}: a watermark aligned to the preserved invariants survives at rate
$\Rstar$, the capacity of the invariant sub-channel. The survivable set is exactly the
non-nullspace of $\A$. On LibriSpeech, a surface mark and AudioSeal collapse under lossy
resynthesis (mel-spectrogram inversion, low-rate EnCodec) while an invariant-aligned mark survives and
degrades gracefully as the channel keeps less; post-laundering detectability is a single
monotone function of the invariant-energy fraction, as the converse predicts.
\end{abstract}

\begin{keywords}
audio watermarking, generative AI, provenance, data-processing inequality, hypothesis testing
\end{keywords}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
Generative speech models make audio provenance urgent, and \emph{watermarking} is the leading
answer: imperceptibly embed a key-dependent signal, later detect it to attribute or flag
synthetic audio~\cite{sanroman2024audioseal,chen2023wavmark}. A now-standard finding is that
these schemes are fragile to \emph{regeneration}: passing watermarked audio through a neural
vocoder, neural codec, or voice-conversion (VC) model drives detection to
chance~\cite{oreilly2025shallow,liu2025sok}. This is not incidental noise---it is the natural
operation of any generative model, and it defeats watermarks that survive conventional
distortions (noise, MP3, resampling).

The phenomenon is thoroughly \emph{measured} but, for audio, not \emph{explained}. For images
this is well understood: Zhao \emph{et al.}~\cite{zhao2024invisible} prove regeneration removes
pixel-space watermarks and prescribe embedding in preserved \emph{semantic} content, and
semantic/latent-space image watermarks (Tree-Ring~\cite{wen2023treering}, Gaussian
Shading~\cite{yang2024gaussianshading}) realize exactly that. What is missing---and what we
provide---is a \emph{quantitative theory for speech}: a matched converse and achievability that
pin down not just \emph{that} surface marks die but \emph{how much} detectability survives and at
what \emph{rate} $\Rstar$, exploiting the concrete, estimable invariants of audio
analysis--resynthesis (content, speaker, prosody) and the phase/masking geometry specific to
hearing.

\noindent\textbf{Contributions.}
(i) We cast blind generative laundering as a \emph{resynthesis channel} $\W=\mathcal S\circ\A$
(\S\ref{sec:model}).
(ii) A \emph{converse} (Thm.~\ref{thm:converse}): watermark energy in $\ker\A$ has zero
surviving detection exponent---a data-processing inequality that explains the collapse,
sharpened for audio by phase/masking.
(iii) A matched \emph{achievability} (Thm.~\ref{thm:achieve}): an invariant-aligned watermark
survives at rate $\Rstar$, the invariant sub-channel capacity; the survivable set is exactly
the non-nullspace of $\A$.
(iv) Validation on real speech (\S\ref{sec:exp}): across analysis--resynthesis channels of
increasing nullspace (STFT and mel-spectrogram inversion, and an EnCodec codec), a surface mark
and AudioSeal collapse when their energy lies in the channel's nullspace, while an invariant
mark survives and degrades gracefully as the preserved subspace shrinks; post-laundering
detectability is monotone in the invariant-energy fraction, as the converse predicts.

\section{The resynthesis channel}
\label{sec:model}
A watermark embeds a key-dependent perturbation $x\mapsto x+\delta_\kappa$ into a host signal
$x$. A detector that knows the key $\kappa$ decides $H_0$ (clean, law $P_0$) vs.\ $H_1$
(watermarked, law $P_1$). The optimal Bayesian error after $N$ i.i.d.\ looks decays as
$e^{-N\,C(P_0,P_1)}$ with the \emph{Chernoff information}
$C(P_0,P_1)=\max_{s\in[0,1]}-\log\int p_0^{1-s}p_1^{s}$~\cite{chernoff1952measure,cover2006elements};
$C=0$ iff $P_0=P_1$, i.e.\ no detector beats chance at any $N$.

\noindent\textbf{Attacker.} A blind launderer applies $\W=\mathcal S\circ\A$: analysis
$\A\!:x\mapsto z$ extracts perceptual invariants $z$ (a near-sufficient statistic for
perceptual identity---two signals with equal $z$ sound the same), and synthesis
$\mathcal S\!:z\mapsto\hat x$ regenerates a waveform consistent with $z$, drawing everything
$\A$ discarded from the clean prior $P_0(\cdot\mid z)$. The attacker does not know $\kappa$ and
does not optimize against the scheme (\S\ref{sec:disc}). Imperceptibility is a budget
$\rho(\delta)\le D$ for a psychoacoustic distortion $\rho$.

\noindent\textbf{Linear--Gaussian surrogate.} To obtain exact constants we work in whitened
coordinates where $P_0=\N(0,I_n)$ (perceptual weighting is carried by a masking metric
$M\!\succ\!0$, $\rho(\delta)=\delta^\top M\delta$). Let $\A\in\R^{k\times n}$ have full row
rank, $P=\A^{+}\A$ the orthogonal projector onto $\mathrm{row}(\A)$ (the \emph{invariant
subspace}), and $I\!-\!P$ the projector onto $\ker\A$ (the \emph{surface subspace} $\mathcal S$
resamples). Then
\begin{equation}
\W(x)=Px+(I-P)w,\qquad w\sim\N(0,I_n)\ \text{fresh,}
\label{eq:channel}
\end{equation}
so $\W_\#\N(0,I)=\N(0,I)$ and $\W_\#\N(\delta,I)=\N(P\delta,I)$: the only surviving part of an
embedded shift is its invariant component $P\delta$. (The first clause of
Thm.~\ref{thm:converse} needs no Gaussianity---Chernoff information obeys the DPI for any
channel; the surrogate supplies constants and the achievable construction.)

\section{Converse: what is erased}
\label{sec:converse}
\begin{theorem}[Nullspace erasure]
\label{thm:converse}
For the channel \eqref{eq:channel} and any watermark $\delta$,
$C(\W_\#P_0,\W_\#P_1)=\tfrac18\norm{P\delta}^2\le\tfrac18\norm{\delta}^2=C(P_0,P_1)$, with
equality iff $\delta\in\mathrm{row}(\A)$. In particular, if $\delta\in\ker\A$ then
$C(\W_\#P_0,\W_\#P_1)=0$: after laundering no detector beats chance, for any $N$. Moreover
$\W(x+\delta)\stackrel{d}{=}\W(x)$, so the laundered watermarked signal is distributed
identically to a laundered clean one.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
$\W_\#P_1=\N(P\delta,I)$ and $\W_\#P_0=\N(0,I)$ share covariance $I$, so their Chernoff
information is $\tfrac18(P\delta)^\top(P\delta)$ (optimum at $s=\tfrac12$). $P$ is an orthogonal
projector, hence $\norm{P\delta}\le\norm{\delta}$, giving the DPI bound, tight on
$\mathrm{row}(\A)$. If $\delta\in\ker\A$ then $P\delta=0$ and the two output laws coincide; and
$\W(x+\delta)=Px+(I-P)w=\W(x)$ in distribution.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why the nullspace is huge for audio]
Hearing is largely phase-insensitive and subject to masking, so a vast set of perturbations is
inaudible; vocoders and codecs discard exactly these (they re-derive phase, quantize masked
bins). Thus the perceptually cheapest embedding directions lie in $\ker\A$. Post-hoc
watermarks, optimized for imperceptibility, drift there---and die. This is the theory's
account of the empirical collapse~\cite{oreilly2025shallow,liu2025sok}.
\end{remark}

\section{Achievability: what survives}
\label{sec:achieve}
Spending the budget on a preserved invariant instead of the nullspace lets the watermark ride
through $\mathcal S$.
\begin{theorem}[Invariant sub-channel]
\label{thm:achieve}
Under the budget, the best surviving exponent is
$\max_{\delta^\top M\delta\le D}\tfrac18\norm{P\delta}^2=\tfrac{D}{8}\lambda_{\max}(P,M)$, at the
top generalized eigenvector of $(P,M)$; it is $0$ when $\delta\in\ker\A$. For payload, encoding a message as a surviving shift $u=P\delta\in\mathrm{row}(\A)$
and facing the clean host as interference ($y=u+\eta$, $\eta\sim\N(0,I)$) gives surviving rate
\begin{equation}
\Rstar=\max_{\tr(M_{\mathrm{row}}Q)\le D}\tfrac12\log\det(I+Q),
\label{eq:rstar}
\end{equation}
a water-filling over the eigenmodes of $M_{\mathrm{row}}=V_{\mathrm{row}}^\top M V_{\mathrm{row}}$.
$\Rstar>0$ whenever $D>0$; a nullspace watermark has $\Rstar=0$ after $\W$.
\end{theorem}
Theorem~\ref{thm:converse} caps the survivable set at $\mathrm{row}(\A)$;
Theorem~\ref{thm:achieve} achieves a positive rate on it. \emph{The survivable set is exactly
the non-nullspace of $\A$, of size $\Rstar$.}

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:exp}
All theorems are cross-checked against Monte-Carlo before use; code reproduces every number.

\noindent\textbf{Surrogate (validation).} Fig.~\ref{fig:surrogate} instantiates
\eqref{eq:channel} with random invariant subspaces. As a watermark drifts from the invariant
subspace into $\ker\A$, post-laundering AUC falls from ${>}0.88$ to $0.50$ (chance), matching
the closed form to RMSE $0.001$; the surviving detection exponent and rate $\Rstar$ are
positive for the invariant-aligned mark and identically zero for the post-hoc (nullspace) mark.

\noindent\textbf{Real speech.} We use \audioNutt{} LibriSpeech test-clean
utterances~\cite{panayotov2015librispeech} at 16\,kHz. Attackers instantiate $\W$ with
\emph{increasing nullspace}: a full linear-STFT Griffin--Lim~\cite{griffin1984signal} (a
near-lossless \emph{control}: $\ker\A$ is only the phase), a mel-spectrogram Griffin--Lim
(lossy: $\ker\A$ is phase \emph{plus} all sub-mel detail), and EnCodec~\cite{defossez2022encodec}
round-trips at $6/3/1.5$\,kbps (nullspace grows as the rate drops). Watermarks at matched SNR
($\snrInv$\,dB): a \emph{surface} mark (fine high-band carrier, waveform-correlation detector,
in $\ker\A$), an \emph{invariant} mel-envelope mark (the constructive realization of
Thm.~\ref{thm:achieve}), and \emph{AudioSeal}, a deployed neural
watermark~\cite{sanroman2024audioseal}. Each mark's invariant-energy fraction $f$ (STFT-magnitude
change captured by the mel envelope) is a pure signal-geometry analog of
$\norm{P\delta}^2/\norm{\delta}^2$.

Table~\ref{tab:e1} gives Part~A, and it sweeps exactly the nullspace-size axis. The STFT
control has the \emph{smallest} nullspace (phase only): it already erases the phase-based
surface mark (AUC $\to 0.5$) yet preserves both magnitude-domain marks. Enlarging the nullspace
to phase \emph{plus} sub-mel detail (mel-spectrogram inversion) additionally collapses AudioSeal
(AUC $1.00\!\to\!\aucAudiosealMel$, bit-accuracy $\bitaccAudiosealMel$; the sub-$0.5$ AUC is a
detector sign-flip, detection destroyed either way), while the invariant mel mark is untouched
($\aucInvMelBefore{}\!\to\!\aucInvMel$). Shrinking the invariant subspace further with low-rate
EnCodec then \emph{gracefully erodes} even the invariant mark ($0.74/0.67/0.60$ at
$6/3/1.5$\,kbps)---the behaviour of $\Rstar$ in Thm.~\ref{thm:achieve} as the channel keeps
less. Here $f$ is measured against the \emph{mel} analysis map, so it predicts the mel-inversion
outcomes; each attacker has its own preserved subspace (a channel-relative $f_\W$), which is why
AudioSeal ($f=\fracAudioseal$, little energy in the mel envelope) resists the EnCodec it was
hardened against yet dies under mel-inversion, whose nullspace contains the phase and fine
detail it relies on. The invariant mark ($f=\fracInv$) sits in the mel-preserved subspace. Part~B makes this quantitative: sweeping a
mixture from surface to invariant, post-laundering AUC is a monotone function of $f$, well
described by the converse's $\sqrt f$ form $\Phi(a\sqrt f+b)$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:e1})---near chance
until $f$ exceeds the surface floor, then rising as the preserved invariant energy grows, as
Thm.~\ref{thm:converse} predicts.

\section{Discussion and scope}
\label{sec:disc}
The converse targets a \emph{blind, non-adaptive} attacker running a generic resynthesizer;
this matches the empirical-collapse literature and yields the clean DPI. A key-aware attacker
that optimizes to remove our specific mark is an arms race we do not claim to win---we treat it
as a stress test, not a core result. Real analysis maps are only approximately sufficient and
$\mathcal S$ is stochastic; our claims degrade gracefully as these idealizations break. Two
honest limitations: marks are matched by SNR rather than a perceptual metric (PESQ/ViSQOL), so
part of the invariant mark's survival may be bought with audibility; and our synthesizers are
spectral-inversion and a neural codec---a learned neural vocoder and voice conversion would test
the converse in its hardest regime. The practical message is prescriptive: \emph{a watermark
survives generative laundering only insofar as it perturbs the invariants the generator is built
to preserve}---the regime where imperceptibility is hardest, quantified by $\Rstar$.

\section{Relation to prior work}
Empirical audio watermarks~\cite{sanroman2024audioseal,chen2023wavmark} and attack/SoK
studies~\cite{oreilly2025shallow,liu2025sok} document the collapse; we explain it and bound
what survives. In the image domain the story is understood: Zhao \emph{et
al.}~\cite{zhao2024invisible} prove removability and advocate semantic embedding, and
Tree-Ring~\cite{wen2023treering} and Gaussian Shading~\cite{yang2024gaussianshading} realize
regeneration-robust semantic watermarks. Our contribution is not the qualitative
``embed-in-what-survives'' idea but a \emph{quantitative, provable} matched converse and
achievability with an explicit surviving \emph{rate} $\Rstar$ for the \emph{speech} resynthesis
channel, tied to its concrete, measurable invariants. Classical information-theoretic
watermarking~\cite{moulin2003information,costa1983writing,chen2001quantization} treats fixed
additive attacks with informed embedding; our attacker is a nonlinear manifold projection and
our detector is blind, locating survivability in the geometry of $\A$.

\section{Conclusion}
Speech watermarks survive generative laundering exactly to the extent they live in the
invariant subspace of the resynthesis channel. We proved a matched converse and achievability
and confirmed on spectral-inversion and neural-codec channels that detection collapses as the surviving
energy vanishes. Designing watermarks \emph{in} the invariant sub-channel, at rate $\Rstar$, is
the path to laundering-robust provenance.
